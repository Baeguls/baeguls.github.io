June 2
On sobriety
It’s my second day being sober from weed and alcohol. Its hard to gauge how much I was using before. I was high multiple times a week during the school year, especially around Alex, and I was drinking at least once or twice a week. I can probably count the time that I was drunk or high (often both) as “lost,” but there was also a much more insidious effect of perpetually being hungover that reached across my entire freshman year of college. I never had the energy to come to class on time, I never had a satisfactory amount of creative output, and I couldn't hold down a job. Maybe the whole year was lost. I am more powerful when I am sober? Some things I am noticing with a sober, well-rested bran: Mess bothers me more. Cleaning is more enticing. I want things to be in order so cooking feels more frictionless and the bugs stay away. I can also write without music. The boredom I expected to feel was replaced by purpose, hopefully eventually creativity. Maybe you could turn this into a video. “Sober” from the frat boy drugs, lol. Bit dramatic. Maybe include your screen time. “App block” has a good tracker for this. On the struggles of early adulthood
It’s so hard to cook every day! I buy too many groceries, I buy groceries I don't want to eat, I don't do my dishes, I generate too many dishes, I need to build a whole spice rack from scratch, I need to build a whole pantry from scratch.

I feel amazing. I’m reducing my screen time, cleaning my apartment, listening to new audiobooks. Not much creative output, but I feel like I’m on the verge of some great things. I have ideas and the will to create them. I’m thinking so clearly. I can’t wait for all my friends to come back to NYC. I have a feeling I am going to be surrounded by cool people. Amandine, Charles, Davids, Jacquie, Columbia people, Stern people, Tisch people. I’ll have disposable income to appreciate a mostly sober life and I’ll build an amazing wardrobe. This is going to be the best year of my life. I’m going to make so many friends and have a great network. When I renegotiate this sobriety contract and resume drinking and smoking, I will do it in moderation. Charles is a perfect role model. Drinking once every two weeks, smoking once every two months. The best part: I have the whole summer, isolated, to work my way up to server and prove myself creatively. The calm before the storm. I’m so glad I stayed in NYC. I have feelings of resentment toward my old friends that I can’t explain. I wish I could show them this life I’m building. I wish they could see that I am the role model. Cutting back on my vices, embracing my weird authentic self, taking a risk and moving to a new city, looking to explore the world and carve out my freedom as soon as possible. I fear what they’ll say when I ascend. He’s a fake friend. He thinks he’s better than us. He didn’t want to talk to us. I will be like a wind chime tossed around in a storm, singing because I can’t help it. I am tremendously lucky. For now, I am different from them, only because of the weather here.

June 8
On Living in NYC
I think New York is the greatest city on earth because it is the most involved city on Earth. Living here is like having a really hot, really high maintenance girlfriend. Shes wants all your money and she’s going to make you wait 2 hours for her to get ready, but when you get to make love to her, oh my god— you know what they say about New York nines? In the suburbs, where I grew up, everything is frictionless. You can drive to Costco, you don’t have to talk to anyone all day, you always have air conditioning, and here, it smells like piss. But EVERY SO OFTEN, you can really make love to this place. Like, LORDE WILL JUST RANDOMLY SHOW UP IN THE PARK AND PERFORM.
That’s New York, man. It won't happen anywhere else.
