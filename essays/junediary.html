I use my bong as a flower pot

every time I’m home, i feel like I’m fighting a past version of myself from a time when everything was so easy. there’s lots of food in the fridge, and old friends around that want to drink, and my mom does my laundry, and my dad has a bike for me with a waxed chain. gas in the car. cheap weed. fresh sheets on the bed.


when i try to impose the new order, waking up at a certain time, starting creative projects, drinking coffee instead of smoking weed, it feels so wrong. it pales in comparison to the memories i made here. the careless days, the fun we had. it feels wrong to work. it compounds the loneliness. how can i see anyone here, and explain these things to them, without feeling like a loser. that i am trying to change. that i don’t want to partake in those bad habits anymore. that i really want to sit in a cafe and talk about the future, about culture. that i want to film youtube videos and make tiktoks and spend a long time in the library doing homework and applying to jobs.


I use my bong as a flower pot.


i have this vision of what my life could be. kickflips in baggy basquiat suits, interesting furniture under industrial ceilings. long nights drinking coffee, writing. mornings at gallery openings. eccentric friends, rich friends, gay friends. an internet presence. friends to make tiktoks with. crush on a coworker. community. purpose. prestige. and it’s going to happen


but why does the path there feel so lonely!??? why does it struggle against the heft of nostalgia? why do i feel, especially when i am home, that the best days are behind me.


in the age of the internet, it’s so hard to detach myself from the past. everyone is still apart of my life, supposedly. They’re there on instagram. but they’re ghosts. they don’t really live here anymore. they’ve changed too, gone further down their own paths, off in a thousand different directions. most of us will never see each other again. but yet, these people haunt me. we see each other’s stories, still get a bump of oh hey, she used to like me, maybe we’ll rekindle that, but none of it matters. and it’s especially obvious when I’m home. when i go to the beach and don’t see a single face I can recognize. instagram is the illusion of social abundance, the illusion of continuity, when in reality everything has changed and nothing will be the same


i will never see most of these people again. if i did, we would perform older versions of ourselves, the safe versions from high school. if we had it in our guts, we could show off who we’ve become, but then… what if we aren’t that similar anymore? what if it’s better to leave each other alone? almost always, it is better to move on


and so, the question becomes, why do i let old people validate the new versions of myself? why am i using the people who saw me puke in taran’s back yard, and who saw christina and i rocking my honda civic, etc, have any say in the person i am becoming? they liked parts of me i now disavow.


social media has numbed me to the direness of my situation. it scratches just enough of the itch that you don't go find the real thing. i need to make new friends! the adventure most continue
